The Science

Four Processes

Every coffee in Chapter I is shaped by a precise fermentation process. Each method was developed over years by Diego Samuel Bermúdez at Finca El Paraiso — and each produces a completely different sensory world from the same origin.

Process 01

Thermal Shock Washed

Used in: La Luna · Sunset
01
Harvest & Ozone Wash

Overripe cherries harvested at peak tannin levels. Disinfected with ozone before fermentation.

02
Temperature Cycling

Rapid alternation between high and low temperatures during fermentation — inducing cellular stress that drives extraordinary flavour precursors into the seed.

03
Culture Medium

Specific microorganisms cultivated from the pulp and mucilage are reintroduced under pressure, fixing the aroma precursors to the bean.

04
Dehumidifier Drying

Dried in a dehumidifier — preserving soft notes, stopping metabolic processes, preventing over-oxidation.

Process 02

Double Anaerobic

Used in: Sweet Garden · Plum Secret
01
Cherry Fermentation

Whole cherries are submerged in sealed tanks with ozone disinfection and fermented anaerobically for 36 hours — the first of two anaerobic stages.

02
Yeast Paraiso Red Fruit

Proprietary red fruit yeast culture is introduced during pulping, driving intense red berry, cherry and plum precursors into the seed.

03
Second Fermentation

The culture medium is re-added to tanks for a second 12-hour anaerobic cycle under pressure, doubling the flavour intensity.

04
Thermal Seal

A thermal shock seals the precursors to the bean before slow dehumidifier drying. Result: deep red fruit, prolonged cherry finish.

Process 03

Lactic

Used in: The Cake
01
Lactobacillus Medium

Cherries are submerged for 72 hours in water with Lactobacillus in milk culture medium — creating a lactic acid-forward environment rarely used in coffee.

02
Kluveromyces Lactis

A specialist dairy yeast introduces coconut milk, cinnamon and blossom precursors — the origin of The Cake's distinctive dessert-like profile.

03
Washed with Special Fermentation

The leachates from cherry fermentation are reintroduced during pulping to further amplify the creamy, spiced notes.

04
Dehumidifier Drying

Slow precision drying locks in the prolonged coconut milk finish. Acidity: lactic. Body: round and heavy.

Process 04

Aerobic + Anaerobic

Used in: Sunset (Y-05 profile)
01
Optimum Ripeness Harvest

Cherries harvested at precise ripeness for balanced sugar and acid content. Ozone disinfected before fermentation begins.

02
72-Hour Anaerobic Stage

First stage in sealed water tanks — no oxygen present. Yeast Paraiso Yellow Fruit drives tropical fruit and peach precursors into the bean.

03
Aerobic Transition

Oxygen is reintroduced in a controlled environment for the second stage — developing caramel, apple and starfruit complexity on top of the anaerobic base.

04
Thermal Shock Seal

Thermal shock fixes the precursors. Result: prolonged apricot aftertaste, malic acidity, creamy body.

Origin · Chapter I

Finca
El Paraiso

Every single coffee in Chapter I comes from one place: Finca El Paraiso in Colombia's Valle del Cauca. Run by Diego Samuel Bermúdez — one of the most decorated and radical producers in specialty coffee — this farm is where the impossible becomes a cup.

Bermúdez pioneered multiple fermentation processes — Thermal Shock, Double Anaerobic, Lactic — each engineered for a specific outcome. The result is five completely different coffees from the same origin, the same season, the same obsessive hands.
