The Science

Four Processes

Every coffee in Chapter I is shaped by a fermentation process developed by Diego Samuel Bermúdez at Finca El Paraiso. Four processes, eight coffees, one farm.

Process 01

Anaerobic Thermal Shock

Used in: La Luna · Letty
01
Ozone Wash + Anaerobic Tank

Cherries disinfected with ozone, then submerged in sealed anaerobic tanks — no oxygen, maximum precursor development.

02
Temperature Cycling

Rapid shifts between high and low temperatures drive extraordinary aromatic compounds deep into the seed.

03
Culture Medium Under Pressure

Microorganisms from mucilage reintroduced under pressure, permanently fixing aroma precursors to the bean.

04
Dehumidifier Drying

Precision drying preserves soft notes. No over-oxidation, no overfermentation.

Process 02

Double Anaerobic

Used in: Sweet Garden · Plum Secret
01
First Fermentation — 36h

Whole cherries submerged in sealed tanks. Anaerobic fermentation 36 hours — the first of two stages.

02
Yeast Paraiso Red Fruit

Proprietary red fruit yeast introduced during pulping — driving cherry, plum and berry precursors into the seed.

03
Second Fermentation — 12h

Culture medium added back under pressure for a second 12-hour anaerobic cycle, doubling flavour intensity.

04
Thermal Seal + Drying

Thermal shock seals precursors before slow dehumidifier drying. Result: deep red fruit, prolonged cherry finish.

Process 03

Lactic

Used in: The Cake
01
Lactobacillus Medium — 72h

Cherries submerged in water with Lactobacillus in milk culture medium — lactic acid fermentation rare in coffee.

02
Kluveromyces Lactis

Dairy yeast introduces coconut milk, cinnamon and blossom precursors — the origin of The Cake's dessert profile.

03
Leachate Reintroduction

Fermentation leachates added back during pulping to amplify the creamy, spiced character further.

04
Precision Drying

Slow drying locks in prolonged coconut milk finish. Acidity: lactic. Body: round and heavy.

Process 04

Double Aerobic Thermal Shock

Used in: Sunset · Little Shock · Little Moment
01
Optimum Ripeness Harvest

Cherries at precise ripeness, ozone disinfected immediately. Balanced sugars and acid from the start.

02
Aerobic + Anaerobic Stages

Two-stage fermentation: tropical notes develop aerobically, then deepened in anaerobic environment.

03
Specialist Yeast

Yeast Paraiso Yellow Fruit or cardamom seeds (Little Shock) drive specific aromatics — lychee, starfruit, black tea.

04
Thermal Shock Seal

Thermal shock fixes precursors before drying. Result: prolonged tropical aftertaste, malic acidity, creamy body.
